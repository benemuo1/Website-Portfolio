The Hours Between
December 2024 · Issue 47
There is a country between waking and sleep where the clocks have no hands and the light comes in sideways, gold and uncertain, like a promise someone forgot to keep.   I have lived there for years now, counting the dust motes, learning the language of almost — almost touching, almost gone.
— On the threshold of memory and forgetting
Salt
November 2024 · Issue 46
My grandmother kept the sea in a jar on the kitchen windowsill. She said it was just salt, but I could hear the waves when I pressed my ear to the glass.   Now the jar sits on my shelf, and the sea sounds like her voice — quiet, steady, endless.
— For the things we inherit without knowing
